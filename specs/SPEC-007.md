# SPEC-007

# Continuity Guardian Engine

Version

0.1

---

## Purpose

Create a Continuity Guardian Engine for KAVEEP-RO.

The engine protects repository continuity by verifying that every approved evolution remains consistent with the repository's Birth, Essence, Life, History, and approved direction.

Repositories may evolve.

Their identity must not disappear.

---

## Mission

Observe Repository

↓

Compare Identity

↓

Analyze History

↓

Measure Continuity

↓

Detect Drift

↓

Evaluate Governance

↓

Report

↓

Request Approval

---

## Core Rule

Growth is allowed.

Identity loss is not.

Every repository must preserve continuity between its original purpose and its current implementation.

---

## Continuity Model

Continuity is evaluated across five dimensions.

---

### Birth

The repository origin.

Questions:

- Why was this repository created?
- What was the original mission?
- What problem was it intended to solve?

Birth never changes.

---

### Essence

The permanent principles.

Examples:

- Safety-first
- Human approval
- Evidence convergence
- Simulation before execution
- Repository governance

Essence changes only through explicit governance decisions.

---

### Life

Everything that evolves.

Examples:

- Features
- Modules
- Documentation
- Architecture
- Workflows
- Issues
- Pull Requests

Life is expected to grow continuously.

---

### History

The complete historical record.

Includes:

- Commits
- Issues
- Pull Requests
- Releases
- Decisions
- Reviews
- Governance records

History provides evidence of continuity.

---

### Continuity

Continuity connects Birth, Essence, Life, and History.

The repository should still be recognizable as the same repository despite growth.

---

## Continuity Evaluation

The engine should determine:

- Is Birth still respected?
- Is Essence preserved?
- Has Life evolved safely?
- Is History consistent?
- Has repository identity drifted?

---

## Continuity States

### Stable

Repository evolves while preserving identity.

---

### Growing

Repository expands appropriately.

Minor differences are expected.

---

### Diverging

Repository begins moving away from approved direction.

Investigation recommended.

---

### Broken

Repository identity can no longer be reliably connected to its Birth and Essence.

Governance review required.

---

## Identity Drift Detection

Examples:

- Safety-first becomes automation-first.
- Governance repository becomes execution repository.
- Simulation engine begins modifying production systems.
- Human approval is removed.
- Repository mission changes without governance approval.

Identity drift may occur gradually.

Early detection is essential.

---

## Continuity Indicators

Positive indicators:

- Consistent SPEC evolution
- Approved roadmap
- Matching implementation
- Complete documentation
- Preserved safety rules
- Approved governance decisions

Negative indicators:

- Silent mission change
- Missing documentation
- Conflicting SPECs
- History rewriting
- Unapproved architectural shifts
- Safety rule removal

---

## Historical Integrity

The engine should verify:

- Decision history exists.
- Reviews remain traceable.
- Approval records remain available.
- Repository evolution is understandable.

History should remain explainable.

---

## Governance Review

If continuity becomes uncertain, the engine should recommend:

- Human review
- Governance review
- Additional evidence
- Identity verification
- Repository audit

Continuity uncertainty must never be ignored.

---

## Output

The engine should generate a Continuity Report.

The report should include:

- Repository identity summary
- Birth status
- Essence status
- Life status
- History status
- Continuity state
- Drift indicators
- Governance concerns
- Risk level
- Recommended action

---

## Safety Rules

The Continuity Guardian Engine must never:

- Ignore identity drift
- Ignore history inconsistencies
- Treat repository age as continuity
- Treat repository popularity as continuity
- Rewrite historical evidence
- Hide governance concerns
- Recommend unsafe evolution

Continuity is evidence-based.

Not assumption-based.

---

## Acceptance Criteria

SPEC-007 is accepted when KAVEEP-RO clearly defines how repository continuity is preserved across Birth, Essence, Life, History, and future evolution.

It must clearly explain:

- Continuity model
- Continuity evaluation
- Continuity states
- Identity drift detection
- Historical integrity
- Governance review
- Continuity report
- Safety rules
